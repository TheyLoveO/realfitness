Why Stretching is Important

Stretching is a great way to improve flexibility, reduce muscle tension, and prevent injury. It can also help improve posture and reduce stress.

Types of Stretching

There are several different types of stretching, including:

  • Static stretching - involves holding a stretch in a stationary position for a period of time.

    • Static Stretching Routine from Chad Earl

  • Dynamic stretching - involves moving the body through a range of motion to warm up the muscles before exercise.

    • DYNAMIC STRETCHING from Amber Dodzweit on Vimeo.

  • Ballistic stretching - involves bouncing or jerking movements to push a muscle beyond its normal range of motion. This type of stretching is not recommended as it can cause injury.

    • BALLERINA BODY Beginner W1 D5

  • Active stretching - involves holding a position with the help of an opposing muscle group.
  • Passive stretching - involves holding a position with the help of an external force, such as a strap or a partner.

    • Horse Stance: Kinetic Stretching & Passive Range Holds

  • Isometric stretching - involves contracting a muscle while in a stretched position to improve flexibility.

    • 90/90 to 90/90 Isometric Movement Path via Bear Sit Sequence

It's important to choose the right type of stretching for your needs and goals. A combination of different types of stretching may be the most effective.

Tips for Safe Stretching

Stretching can be beneficial, but it's important to do it safely to avoid injury. Here are some tips:

  • Warm up before stretching with some light activity, such as jogging or jumping jacks.
  • Stretch slowly and gently, without bouncing or jerking movements.
  • Breathe deeply and evenly throughout the stretch.
  • Hold each stretch for 10-30 seconds, and repeat each stretch 2-4 times.
  • Don't force a stretch beyond your comfort level.
  • Avoid stretching cold muscles, as this can cause injury.
  • Stop stretching if you feel pain or discomfort.
