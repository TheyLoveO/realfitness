Workouts to build muscle

Dumbbell Bicep Curls

Dumbbell bicep curls are a classic bicep exercise that target your biceps and forearms. Here's how to do it:

  1. Stand with your feet shoulder-width apart and hold a dumbbell in each hand, palms facing forward.
  2. Keep your elbows close to your sides and slowly lift the dumbbells toward your shoulders, rotating your palms as you go.
  3. Pause for a moment at the top, then slowly lower the dumbbells back down to the starting position.
  4. Repeat for the desired number of repetitions.

Barbell Bicep Curls

Barbell bicep curls are another great bicep exercise that target your biceps and forearms. Here's how to do it:

  1. Stand with your feet shoulder-width apart and hold a barbell with an underhand grip, palms facing up.
  2. Keep your elbows close to your sides and slowly lift the barbell toward your shoulders.
  3. Pause for a moment at the top, then slowly lower the barbell back down to the starting position.
  4. Repeat for the desired number of repetitions.

Hammer Curls

Hammer curls are a bicep exercise that also targets your forearms. Here's how to do it:

  1. Stand with your feet shoulder-width apart and hold a dumbbell in each hand, palms facing each other.
  2. Keep your elbows close to your sides and slowly lift the dumbbells toward your shoulders.
  3. Pause for a moment at the top, then slowly lower the dumbbells back down to the starting position.
  4. Repeat for the desired number of repetitions.

Push-Ups

Push-ups are a classic chest exercise that you can do anywhere without any equipment. Here's how to do it:

  1. Get into a plank position with your hands shoulder-width apart.
  2. Lower your body by bending your elbows until your chest almost touches the floor.
  3. Push back up to the starting position.
  4. Repeat for the desired number of repetitions.

Bench Press

The bench press is a popular chest exercise that you can do with a barbell or dumbbells. Here's how to do it:

  1. Lie down on a bench with your feet flat on the floor.
  2. Grasp the barbell or dumbbells with an overhand grip, hands slightly wider than shoulder-width apart.
  3. Lower the barbell or dumbbells to your chest.
  4. Push the barbell or dumbbells back up to the starting position.
  5. Repeat for the desired number of repetitions.

Dumbbell Fly

The dumbbell fly is an isolation exercise that targets your chest muscles. Here's how to do it:

  1. Lie down on a bench with your feet flat on the floor.
  2. Hold a dumbbell in each hand with your arms extended above your chest.
  3. Lower your arms out to the sides, keeping a slight bend in your elbows.
  4. Bring the dumbbells back up above your chest.
  5. Repeat for the desired number of repetitions.

Dumbbell Bench Press

The dumbbell bench press is a great exercise to target your chest and build strength. Here's how to do it:

  1. Lie down on a flat bench with a dumbbell in each hand, resting on your thighs.
  2. Bring the dumbbells up to shoulder level and rotate your wrists so that your palms are facing forward.
  3. Push the dumbbells up and together until your arms are fully extended.
  4. Lower the dumbbells back down to shoulder level.
  5. Repeat for the desired number of repetitions.

Cable Flys

Cable flys are a great exercise to isolate and work your chest muscles. Here's how to do it:

  1. Stand in the center of a cable machine and hold the handles with an overhand grip.
  2. Step forward and bring your arms out to the sides, keeping them slightly bent.
  3. Bring your hands together in front of your chest, squeezing your chest muscles.
  4. Slowly bring your arms back to the starting position.
  5. Repeat for the desired number of repetitions.

Machine Chest Press

The machine chest press is a great exercise to build chest strength and muscle. Here's how to do it:

  1. Sit down on the machine with your back against the pad and your feet flat on the floor.
  2. Grasp the handles and push them forward until your arms are fully extended.
  3. Slowly bring the handles back to the starting position.
  4. Repeat for the desired number of repetitions.

Squats

Squats are a compound exercise that target the muscles in your legs, hips, and glutes. Here's how to do it:

  1. Stand with your feet shoulder-width apart and your toes pointing slightly outward.
  2. Bend your knees and lower your body as if you were sitting back into a chair.
  3. Lower your body until your thighs are parallel to the floor, keeping your knees in line with your toes.
  4. Push through your heels and return to the starting position.
  5. Repeat for the desired number of repetitions.

Lunges

Lunges are a unilateral exercise that target the muscles in your legs and glutes. Here's how to do it:

  1. Stand with your feet hip-width apart and your hands on your hips.
  2. Step forward with your right foot, bending your right knee and lowering your body until your right thigh is parallel to the floor.
  3. Push through your right heel and return to the starting position.
  4. Repeat with your left foot, alternating legs for the desired number of repetitions.

Leg Curls

Leg curls are a great exercise to isolate and work your hamstrings. Here's how to do it:

  1. Lie face down on a leg curl machine and position the pad on the back of your ankles.
  2. Keeping your hips flat on the bench, curl your legs up towards your buttocks.
  3. Slowly lower your legs back to the starting position.
  4. Repeat for the desired number of repetitions.
